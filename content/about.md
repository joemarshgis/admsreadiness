# About

I’m Joe Marsh, a senior consultant and product leader focused on helping utilities modernize operations through GIS, ADMS, utility network, and grid management solutions.

My work sits at the intersection of technology, strategy, and operations. I’m especially interested in how strong network data, clear system ownership, and practical architecture decisions improve reliability, planning, and operational performance.

Over the course of my career, I’ve worked on utility transformation efforts involving ADMS implementations, Utility Network migrations, outage management improvements, GIS data modeling, training, and business intelligence. I care most about turning complex technical environments into clear, usable outcomes for the people who operate them every day.

Earlier in my career, I also had the opportunity to help shape utility-focused product direction and translate industry needs into practical solutions. That experience reinforced a perspective I still bring to every engagement: technology only works when it aligns with real operational needs.

## What I Write About

On this site, I write about topics like:

- GIS as a system of record
- ADMS and OMS data readiness
- Utility architecture and integration strategy
- Grid modernization
- Operational data quality
- Utility Network and enterprise transformation

## Why This Site Exists

This site is a place to share practical thinking on utility systems, GIS, and operational readiness.

A lot of conversations in this space focus on applications, platforms, or implementation checklists. I’m more interested in the underlying model: the data, ownership boundaries, operating processes, and architectural decisions that determine whether those systems actually succeed.
