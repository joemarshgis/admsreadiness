# About

I’m Joe Marsh, a senior consultant and product leader focused on helping utilities modernize operations through GIS, ADMS, utility network, and grid management solutions. I write about GIS, ADMS, and digital grid modernization, focusing on what it really takes to get complex utility technology programs into production. My perspective comes from product leadership at Esri and delivery work across major and mid‑sized utilities, including PG&E, Con Edison, and others navigating large‑scale grid modernization.

My work sits at the intersection of technology, strategy, and operations. I’m especially interested in how strong network data, clear system ownership, and practical architecture decisions improve reliability, planning, and operational performance for real operators and program leaders.

Over the course of my career, I’ve worked on utility transformation efforts involving ADMS implementations, Utility Network migrations, outage management improvements, GIS data modeling, training, and business intelligence. That has included multi‑year ADMS and GIS programs at PG&E and Con Edison, along with migrations, training, and integration work for a range of other utilities. I care most about turning complex technical environments into clear, usable outcomes for the people who operate them every day.

At PG&E, I helped deliver a three‑year ADMS effort spanning 760 substations with a core team of about 20 specialists, supporting a system that serves roughly 16 million people across a 70,000‑square‑mile service area and is pursuing a major undergrounding program to reduce wildfire risk. At Con Edison, I supported a major ADMS and GIS upgrade in one of the most operationally dense urban environments in North America, where vertically integrated operations and highly accurate customer‑to‑meter relationships are critical.

Earlier in my career, I had the opportunity to help shape utility‑focused product direction and translate industry needs into practical solutions as a Product Owner at Esri and as a consultant at POWER Engineers. That experience reinforced a perspective I still bring to every engagement: technology only works when it aligns with real operational needs, data realities, and the way utilities actually run their systems.

## What I Write About

On this site, I write about topics like:

- GIS as a system of record
- ADMS and OMS data readiness
- Utility architecture and integration strategy
- Grid modernization
- Operational data quality
- Utility Network and enterprise transformation
- The human and organizational side of getting utility technology into production

## Why This Site Exists

This site is a place to share practical thinking on utility systems, GIS, and operational readiness.

A lot of conversations in this space focus on applications, platforms, or implementation checklists. I’m more interested in the underlying model: the data, ownership boundaries, operating processes, and architectural decisions that determine whether those systems actually succeed.
